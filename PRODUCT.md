# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, founders, journalists, conference organizers, and potential collaborators encountering Lane Rettig for the first time, typically while evaluating him for a role, a partnership, a talk, or a story. They're skimming, not reading deeply on the first pass — scanning for "who is this person, what have they done, do they matter to me."

## Product Purpose

Replace a traditional CV/resume with a self-controlled narrative: a single page that presents career, writing, talks, and current focus cohesively, on Lane's own terms, so the first impression is the one he intends rather than whatever a LinkedIn scrape or outdated bio produces. Success is a visitor leaving with a clear, accurate, memorable sense of the throughline across healthcare tech, blockchain/crypto governance, and AI — "putting the agent back in user agent."

## Brand Personality

Clean, credible, understated. Quiet confidence, not a pitch deck. Voice is direct and occasionally dry (see the D. E. Shaw aside) rather than promotional. No marketing-speak, no SaaS energy.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS/startup landing page tropes: hero metrics, gradient text, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows, numbered section markers, identical icon-card grids
- Anything that reads as a sales pitch rather than a personal record
- Visual noise that competes with the content — this is a reading experience, not a showcase

## Design Principles

- Content carries the page; layout and typography stay out of the way
- Every claim should be a link to primary evidence (the article, the talk recording, the org) wherever possible
- Narrative coherence over completeness — curated highlights with a path to the fuller record, not exhaustive lists
- Plain language over jargon, except where a deliberate, explained exception (the tagline) is doing narrative work
- Dry wit is welcome in small doses; it should never undercut credibility

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline practice: sufficient color contrast, semantic HTML, alt text on images. No additional WCAG level specified.
